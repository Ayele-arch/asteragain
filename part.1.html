INTRODUCTION

Human rights are rights inherent to all human beings, regardless of race, sex, nationality, ethnicity, language, religion, or any other status. Human rights include the right to life and liberty, freedom from slavery and torture, freedom of opinion and expression, the right to work and education, and many more. Everyone is entitled to these rights, without discrimination. A milestone document which is known as universal declaration of human rights was drifted by different representation from all of the whole world.
The Declaration was proclaimed by the United Nations General Assembly in Paris on 10 December 1948 by General Assembly as a common standard of achievements for all peoples and all nations.

WHAT IS HUMAN RIGHT?

Human rights are rights we have simply because we exist as human beings - they are not granted by any state.
These universal rights are inherent to us all, regardless of nationality, sex, national or ethnic origin, color, religion, language, or any other status. They range from the most fundamental as, the right to life, the right to be healthy, the right to own property, the right to have access for education, the right to do work can be mentioned.

WHAT DO WE MEAN WHEN WE SAY HUMAN RUGHT IS UNIVERSAL AND INALIENABLE?

The principle of universality of human right is the cornerstone of international human rights law. This means we all human beings are equally entitled to our human rights. Human rights are inalienable. They should not be taken away from anyone except in specific situation.For example, - If a person found guilty in a murder case then according to the legal law of a country from the person, they will take away his/her rights of liberty.

WHAT DO WE MEAN WHEN WE SAY HUMAN RIGHTS IS INDIVISBLE AND INTERDEPENDENT?

All human rights are indivisible and interdependent. This means all kinds of rights cannot be enjoyed fully without other. For example, violating economic, cultural, social rights can negatively affect our other rights.

BOTH RIGHTS AND OBLIGATION

States have obligation and duties under international law to respect, protect and fulfill human rights.

  • The obligation to respect means that state must refrain from interfering with or curtailing the enjoyment of human rights.
  • The obligation to protect requires states to protect individual and groups against human right abuse.
  • The obligation to fulfill requires states to take positive action to facilitate the enjoyment of basic human rights.
